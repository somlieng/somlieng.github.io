Jane Goodall

is a world expert on chimpanzees. She is a british primatologist, ethologist, anthropologist, UN messenger of Peace, and a member of the board of nonhuman rights project since its founding in 1996. Social and Family interaction study of chimpanzees (55 year long study) in Gombe Stream Park, Tanzania

Her fondness for animals was clear very eary on in her life. She once hid in a henhouse watching hens lays eggs for so long that her family reported her missing to the police. She also writes in her book, Reason for Hope, "My father gave me a lifelike chimpanzee doll. My mother;s friends were horrided by this toy, thinking it would frighten me and give me nightmare. My doll, Jubilee, still sits on my chair in London"

Jane couldn’t afford to go to college. So she worked. When she was 22, she got invited by her friend Clo to come stay on her family’s new farm in Kenya. Jane had always wanted to go to the continent of Africa after reading the story “Dr. Dolittle” by Hugh Lofting, which is a story about a doctor who could speak to animals and traveled to the continent of Africa and goes on numerous adventures. Jane was ecstatic and saved up all her money for the round trip passage to Kenya.
She set sail in 1957 and arrived in Mombasa on April 2nd. A few weeks afterwards, she meets Louis B. Leakey. He is a famous archaeologist and paleontologist who was interested in studying about evolution. He was impressed by Jane's energy, interest, and knowledge in animals. He hired her as his assistant and secretary at the Coryndon Museum. Soon, she and another student were helping him and his anthropologist wife, Mary Leakey, dig up fossils in the Olduvai Gorge.
At that time, Leakey was looking for someone to go to Tanzania to study chimpanzees. They were a mystery at the time. Studying them would be fascinating and might provide clues about evolution. Louis offers this chance to Jane and she agrees. Jane goes to Tanzania in 1960 with her mother. British authorities resisted the idea of a young woman living among wild animals in Africa alone.
